--- title: "Research" permalink: /research/ author_profile: true --- ## Working Papers ### Targeted Electricity Subsidies and Rural Development: Evidence from India’s RGGVY Program This paper studies whether targeted electricity subsidies can improve rural welfare outcomes in India. It examines the Rajiv Gandhi Grameen Vidyutikaran Yojana (RGGVY), which provided free electricity connections to Below Poverty Line households. Using India Human Development Survey panel data for 2004–05 and 2011–12 and district-level variation in RGGVY rollout, the paper applies a Difference-in-Differences design based on years of exposure to the programme. Presented at the 20th Annual Conference on Economic Growth and Development, Indian Statistical Institute, New Delhi, December 2025. Accepted for presentation at the 5th Asian Economic Development Conference, Hong Kong; Nordic Conference in Development Economics, Sweden; and Royal Economic Society Annual Conference 2026, Newcastle University, UK. ### Rural Electrification and the Demographic Transition in India This paper estimates the long-run impact of rural electrification on fertility and maternal health behaviour using NFHS-4 and NFHS-5 microdata. It applies Two-Way Fixed Effects, Instrumental Variables, and Difference-in-Differences approaches based on the rollout of RGGVY. The study examines channels such as media exposure, maternal healthcare access, institutional deliveries, and desired family size. Presented at the 7th Annual Economics Conference, Ahmedabad University, January 2026. ### Does Digital Identification Improve Disability Welfare Delivery? Evidence from the Integration of the Unique Disability ID System in India This paper evaluates whether digital identification improves welfare delivery for persons with disabilities. It exploits the staggered rollout of the Unique Disability ID programme using state-district panel data for 2014–2023 and applies a Difference-in-Differences design. Presented at the International Conference on Digitalisation, Market and Society, IISER Mohali, December 2025. ## Academic Projects ### Lives in Transition: Exploring Livelihoods and Service Access in Aarey Colony Guide: Prof. Srijit Mishra This project examined the relationship between infrastructure access and capability deprivation in Aarey Colony, Mumbai. Drawing on the capability approach of Amartya Sen and Martha Nussbaum, it studied access to water, electricity, and basic services among tribal and migrant communities.
